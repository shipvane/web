The difference

Your code, your cloud, your call.

Black-box SaaS agents run your codebase in someone else's opaque environment and hand back code they can't truly verify. Shipvane runs in your cloud and proves its work against your actual build.

Black-box SaaS agents

Hand it over and hope

Your repo runs inside a vendor's sealed environment you can't see or control. The model generates code it never truly runs, guesses at your conventions, and hands you a PR that may not even compile.

Shipvane

Owned, then proven

Shipvane runs agentic AI inside your own AWS account — a real branch, your real build & tests. It reads the actual errors and iterates until the gate is green. Nothing leaves your environment; nothing merges without your say.

The ADLC

One agentic loop. The whole lifecycle.

ADLC — the Agentic Software Development Life Cycle. Every stage a senior team runs, Shipvane Engine runs autonomously across your whole stack — from a raw ticket to a merged PR, all inside your own cloud. Point it once; it sails the entire voyage.

1

Intake & triage

A ticket or idea lands. Engine reads it alongside your repo to scope the real work.

2

Enrichment

Sharpens the ticket — acceptance criteria, affected areas, and labels — so the plan is grounded.

3

Planning

Designs the change across the stack, in your conventions, before a line is written.

4

Implementation

Writes frontend and backend on a real branch in a fresh checkout in your cloud.

5

Tests

Writes and runs the tests that prove the change actually does what the ticket asked.

6

Real CI gate

Runs your actual build & tests, reads the real errors, and iterates until the gate is green.

7

Pull request

Opens a clean, merge-ready PR — the full diff, ready for review where your code already lives.

✓

Review & merge

You hold the final say. Feedback loops back through the cycle; on your approval, it merges.

A full loop, not a one-shot — review feeds back in and the cycle runs until it's earned the merge.

Two pieces, one workflow

Connect points the way. Engine does the work.

Shipvane is an open, self-hostable stack — a CLI you drive from your terminal and an agent runtime that lives in your cloud. No SaaS tenancy, no lock-in.

Shipvane Connect · CLI

Your terminal, your control surface

Connect is the command-line client you point at a ticket. It hands the work to your Engine, streams progress back, and keeps you in the loop on every approval — all without leaving the shell you already live in.

Shipvane Engine · Runtime

The full-stack ADLC, in your cloud

Engine is the agentic core — the whole Agentic Software Development Life Cycle, packaged to deploy and provision into your own AWS account. It runs the real checkout, build, and CI loop where your code already lives — so you own the environment, the logs, and the keys.
